Can you explain why the expansion ratio of the nozzle is so critical for vacuum engines compared to sea-level ones?

Sent 14:02
auto_awesome

Great question! In a vacuum, there's no ambient pressure to push back against the exhaust. A larger expansion ratio allows the exhaust gases to expand further, converting more internal thermal energy into kinetic energy (velocity).

If we used a sea-level nozzle in space, the gas would still have high pressure as it exits, essentially "wasting" potential thrust. Does that help you visualize the pressure gradient?
