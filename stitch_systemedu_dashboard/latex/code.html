The Nature of Kinetic Friction

Kinetic friction occurs when two objects are moving relative to each other and rub together. This force acts in the opposite direction to the motion.

Unlike static friction, kinetic friction is generally constant regardless of the speed of the object, assuming the surface properties remain consistent during the interaction.

Mathematical Formula

fk = μkcloseN
fk: Kinetic friction force
μk: Friction coefficient
N: Normal force
