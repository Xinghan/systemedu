The Architecture of Neural Networks

At the heart of modern artificial intelligence lies the Neural Network. Inspired by the biological framework of the human brain, these mathematical constructs enable machines to learn from vast amounts of data by identifying complex patterns.

1. The Perceptron Model

Proposed in the 1950s, the perceptron is the simplest form of a neural network. It takes several binary inputs, $x_1, x_2, \dots$, and produces a single binary output.

"The goal of machine learning is not to find a single correct answer, but to find a representation that generalizes across unseen data."

Weights ($w_i$) are assigned to each input, representing their importance in the decision-making process. The output is determined by whether the weighted sum $\sum w_ix_i$ is greater than some threshold value.

2. Deep Layers & Backpropagation

Modern networks stack these perceptrons—now called neurons—into multiple layers: input, hidden, and output layers. The process of tuning the weights is achieved through Gradient Descent and Backpropagation.
